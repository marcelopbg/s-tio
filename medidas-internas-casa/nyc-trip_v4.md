## DAY 1 · July 4 (Friday) — Arrival + 4th of July

**Morning (5:40am–noon)**
- Land at Newark (EWR) ~5:40am
  - Option A (cheapest): NJ Transit train from EWR → Penn Station NYC (~25 min) → 7 train to Flushing (~30 min) = ~1 hour total
  - Option B (easiest at 6am on a holiday): Uber/Lyft directly to Flushing hotel (~45 min, ~$60–70, minimal traffic July 4 morning)
- Check in or drop luggage, freshen up
- **Rest until 11am or noon** — you almost certainly flew overnight, don't fight it

**Afternoon (noon–6pm)**
- Flushing Meadows–Corona Park (10 min walk from most Flushing hotels)
  - Unisphere, open lawns, the USTA tennis complex in the background
  - Zero pressure, no agenda — just decompress and feel the city air
- Late lunch: explore Flushing Main St / Roosevelt Ave for the first time
  - Buddhist vegan spots, dumpling shops, bakeries everywhere

**Evening (6pm–late)**
- Head to DUMBO, Brooklyn
  - 7 train → A/C train toward Brooklyn, exit High St · ~50 min total
  - Arrive by 7pm — it will fill up fast (July 4 is a huge event in NYC)
- Brooklyn Bridge Park waterfront for **Macy's 4th of July Fireworks**
  - Best views of the East River fireworks with the Bridge in frame
  - Fireworks typically start ~9:20pm
  - Bring a snack, sit on the grass, watch the city go insane
- Head back after fireworks — expect packed trains, be patient

## DAY 2 · July 5 (Saturday) — Brooklyn

**Morning (9–11am)**
- Williamsburg brunch — best vegan neighborhood in NYC
  - Bunna Cafe (Ethiopian, fully vegan, 1084 Flushing Ave) — book ahead or arrive early
  - Champs Diner (vegan comfort diner) or just wander Bedford Ave for a coffee + pastry

**Afternoon (11am–5pm)**
- Walk south down Bedford Ave — feel the neighborhood
- Head to **Bushwick Collective** (~20 min walk or short Uber)
  - Open-air street art district — no entry, no schedule, just walk
  - Best blocks: Troutman St, Jefferson St, St. Nicholas Ave
- Walk back through Bushwick toward Williamsburg via Myrtle Ave

**Late afternoon (5–8pm)**
- East River State Park waterfront (North 8th St, Williamsburg)
  - Manhattan skyline at golden hour — one of the best free views in the city
- Grab early dinner on Bedford Ave before heading back

**Evening (8pm+)**
- Optional: stay in Williamsburg for bars
- Or head back to Flushing — rest, you have many days ahead

## DAY 3 · July 6 (Sunday) — Central Park + Upper West Side

**Morning (8–11am) — go early, beat the heat**
- Enter Central Park at 72nd St (B/C train to 72nd St)
- Route: Bethesda Fountain → Sheep Meadow → Reservoir loop → The Ramble
- The Ramble is a wooded area in the middle of the park — strange and peaceful
- Exit at 86th St side by ~11am before the summer heat peaks

**Afternoon (11am–4pm)**
- Upper West Side walk south from 86th St
  - **Zabar's** (2245 Broadway) — legendary NYC deli / grocery, grab something
  - Browse Amsterdam Ave and Columbus Ave — low-key residential NYC texture
- Option: walk through Riverside Park along the Hudson (quieter than Central Park)
- Late lunch: Westside vegan-friendly spots on Amsterdam Ave

**Evening (4–8pm)**
- Option A: walk to the **Met Museum steps on 5th Ave** at 82nd St
  - No need to go in — the steps and the view down 5th Ave are iconic on their own
  - Sitting on those steps watching the city move is quintessential NYC
- Option B: head downtown, explore the Lower East Side
  - Orchard St / Rivington St — good texture, galleries, coffee

## DAY 4 · July 7 (Monday) — Lower Manhattan + Jazz Night

**Morning (9am–noon)**
- **9/11 Memorial** (opens 9am — go early, it gets very crowded)
  - The reflecting pools are genuinely moving
  - Allow 1 hour, maybe more
- Walk Financial District: Stone Street → Bowling Green → the Charging Bull
- Walk toward the East River: Brooklyn Bridge entrance on the Manhattan side

**Afternoon (noon–4pm)**
- **Walk the Brooklyn Bridge** — enter Manhattan side at Centre St
  - Wooden pedestrian walkway, elevated above traffic
  - Takes ~30–40 min to walk across
  - Come out in DUMBO — have a look around, grab lunch
- Head to **Chinatown** (short walk or 1 stop on the subway)
  - Lunch at a Buddhist vegetarian restaurant — Buddha Bodai or Vegetarian Dim Sum House
  - Walk Canal St and the surrounding blocks

**Late afternoon (4–7pm)**
- Head to **West Village** (take the A/C/E to 14th St)
  - Walk: Bleecker St → Christopher St → Hudson River Park
  - This neighborhood has the best human-scale streetscape in NYC

**Evening (7pm–late)**
- Dinner somewhere on Bleecker or in the West Village
- **Jazz — pick one:**
  - **Smalls Jazz Club** (183 W 10th St) — ~$25 cover, sets ~7:30pm and ~10pm, very intimate, cash preferred. Best value jazz in NYC.
  - **Village Vanguard** (178 7th Ave S) — more legendary, ~$35, book ahead for weekend sets

## POST-WORK FREE DAYS · July 13–16

### DAY 10 · July 13 (Saturday) — Governors Island

**Morning (9am–noon)**
- Ferry from Pier 6, Brooklyn Bridge Park (or Whitehall Terminal, Lower Manhattan)
  - Ferries run every 30 min from ~10am, ~$3 each way
  - 7 train → transfer to subway toward Brooklyn, 10 min walk to Pier 6
- **Governors Island** — car-free, no rush
  - Hammock Grove: hundreds of hammocks, open to anyone
  - Nolan Park: art installations and quiet Victorian houses
  - The Hills: elevated views of the **full Manhattan skyline + Statue of Liberty**, completely free, better than any paid rooftop in the city

**Afternoon (noon–4pm)**
- Back to Brooklyn by ~2–3pm
- **Red Hook** walk (short Uber/Lyft from the ferry pier)
  - Waterfront, former industrial neighborhood, weirdly quiet and beautiful
  - Van Brunt St for coffee and a walk

**Evening (7pm–late)**
- **Bushwick — electronic music**
  - **Elsewhere** (599 Johnson Ave) — multi-room venue, check lineup
  - **Bossa Nova Civic Club** (1271 Myrtle Ave) — darker and more underground
  - Pick one based on who's playing — check both calendars the week before

### DAY 11 · July 14 (Sunday) — Roosevelt Island + East Village

**Morning (9–11am)**
- **Roosevelt Island Tram** at 59th St & 2nd Ave (F train to Lexington/63rd, walk to tram)
  - Aerial tram ride over the East River — ~5 min each way, stunning Midtown views
  - Pay with MetroCard (same as subway)
  - Walk the island: Southpoint Park at the tip has a surreal lighthouse and Manhattan views
  - Strange, quiet, not many tourists — worth 2 hours

**Afternoon (11am–5pm)**
- Take the subway down to **East Village** (F train or walk)
  - Lunch: Superiority Burger (430 E 9th St) — legendary, tiny, vegetarian, cheap
  - Walk: Tompkins Square Park → St. Marks Place → 2nd Ave / Ave A
  - Record stores, vintage shops, coffee spots — great texture
  - Less polished than West Village, more interesting

**Evening (5pm+)**
- Dinner in East Village (tons of options, all cheap to mid-range)
- Easy night — you've had a full weekend

## VEGAN FOOD

### Flushing (your base — treat this seriously)
- Buddhist vegan Chinese restaurants on Main St: full mock-meat menus, very cheap
- New World Mall food court (B1 basement): diverse stalls, authentic
- Roosevelt Ave night market energy on weekends
- This is among the best Asian food you'll find anywhere outside Asia

### By neighborhood
| Area | What to look for |

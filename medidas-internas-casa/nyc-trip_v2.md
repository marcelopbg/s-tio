# 🇺🇸 NYC Itinerary — July 4–16 (Solo)

**Base:** Flushing, Queens — 7 train to Midtown (~30 min)
**Work:** July 8–12 (Mon–Fri) at office in Flushing
**Arrival:** July 4, ~5:40am | **Departure:** July 16, 8:30pm

---

## BEFORE YOU GO

- July in NYC: hot and humid — water bottle mandatory
- Sunset after 8:30pm — evenings are very long, use them
- 7 train = your lifeline (Flushing → Times Square → Hudson Yards, direct)
- Flushing food is world-class Asian cuisine — not a consolation prize

---

## PRE-WORK FREE DAYS (July 4–7)

### DAY 1 — July 4 · Arrival + 4th of July

**Morning:** Land ~5:40am → hotel Flushing. Rest until noon — you need it.

**Afternoon:** Flushing Meadows–Corona Park (5-min walk from most Flushing hotels)
- Unisphere, open lawns, zero effort, decompress from flight

**Evening:** DUMBO → Brooklyn Bridge Park for Macy's 4th of July fireworks
- 7 train → transfer to A/C toward Brooklyn, get off at High St
- Arrive DUMBO by 7pm — it fills up fast
- Fireworks over East River with the bridge in frame — once in a lifetime

---

### DAY 2 — July 5 · Brooklyn

**Morning:** Williamsburg brunch (best vegan neighborhood in NYC)
- Bunna Cafe (Ethiopian, full vegan menu), Modern Love, or just wander

**Afternoon:** Walk to Bushwick Collective
- 20 min walk from Williamsburg — massive open-air street art district
- No entry, no schedule, just walk

**Late afternoon:** Back to Williamsburg waterfront
- East River State Park — Manhattan skyline at golden hour

**Evening:** Stay in Williamsburg for bars or head back

---

### DAY 3 — July 6 · Central Park + Upper West Side

**Morning (go early — before 10am):** Central Park from 72nd St entrance
- Route: Reservoir loop → Sheep Meadow → Bethesda Fountain → The Ramble
- Heat builds fast in July — morning is the move

**Afternoon:** Upper West Side walk north of the park
- Zabar's deli (institution — grab something)
- Amsterdam Ave / Columbus Ave for texture

**Evening option:** Met Museum steps at 5th Ave — iconic view, free to sit
- Or rest — tomorrow is a long day

---

### DAY 4 — July 7 · Lower Manhattan + Jazz Night

**Morning:** 9/11 Memorial (open 9am — go early, more peaceful)
- Walk the Financial District: Fulton Center, Stone Street, the Bull

**Midday:** Walk the Brooklyn Bridge from the Manhattan side
- Come back through DUMBO if you want — or continue to Chinatown

**Afternoon:** Chinatown lunch
- Tons of Buddhist vegan restaurants, very cheap, excellent

**Evening:** West Village → Jazz
- Walk Christopher St → Bleecker → Hudson River Park
- Late night: **Smalls Jazz Club** (183 W 10th St)
  - Cover ~$25, sets start ~10pm, cash preferred, intimate and legendary
  - Alt: Village Vanguard (more historic, ~$35, book ahead)

---

## WORK DAYS — July 8–12

You're at the Flushing office with flexibility. Use evenings via the 7 train.
The 7 train runs to Times Square in ~30 min. From there everything is reachable.

---

### MONDAY July 8 · Easy re-entry

**Evening:** Stay local. Flushing food exploration.
- New World Mall food court (basement level — authentic, chaotic, great)
- Buddhist vegan spots on Main St / Roosevelt Ave area
- Low effort. Recover from the first 4 days.

---

### TUESDAY July 9 · High Line

**Evening:** 7 train → Hudson Yards → High Line
- Enter at 34th St (north end), walk south toward Meatpacking District
- This hits at perfect golden hour / sunset in July (~7:30–8:30pm)
- Exit at Chelsea Market level — grab food, walk the market

---

### WEDNESDAY July 10 · West Village + Jazz

**Evening:** 7 train → West Village
- Walk: 14th St west → Hudson River Park → Bleecker St loop
- If you skipped jazz on July 7: **Smalls** or **Village Vanguard** tonight

---

### THURSDAY July 11 · Chelsea

**Evening:** Chelsea galleries (many are free, open until 6–7pm)
- Or: Meatpacking District dinner (moderate budget, good energy at night)
- Or: earlier night — long weekend starts tomorrow

---

### FRIDAY July 12 · MoMA or Nowadays

**Option A — MoMA (open until 9pm on Fridays)**
- ~$30 entry, 7 train to 5th Ave, worth it as a first-timer
- MoMA at night has a completely different vibe — less crowded

**Option B — Nowadays (Ridgewood, Queens)**
- ~20 min from Flushing, outdoor dancefloor, best electronic venue in NYC
- Check calendar: nowadays.nyc — Friday nights are consistently strong
- One of the most unique spaces in the city — close to home base

---

## POST-WORK FREE DAYS (July 13–16)

### DAY 10 — July 13 · Governors Island

**Morning:** Ferry from Pier 6 (Brooklyn Bridge Park) or Whitehall Terminal (Manhattan)
- ~$3 ferry, runs every 30 min on weekends
- Car-free island: art installations, hammock groves, food, unobstructed views of Manhattan skyline + Statue of Liberty
- Better views than any paid rooftop in the city

**Afternoon:** Back to Brooklyn — Red Hook or Carroll Gardens walk

**Evening:** Bushwick — electronic music
- **Elsewhere** (599 Johnson Ave) — multi-room venue, big bookings
- **Bossa Nova Civic Club** (1271 Myrtle Ave) — darker, underground, authentic
- Check both calendars ahead of time

---

### DAY 11 — July 14 · Roosevelt Island + East Village

**Morning:** Roosevelt Island Tram from 59th St & 2nd Ave
- Short aerial tram ride, stunning views of Midtown
- Walk the island — strange, quiet, beautiful edge-of-Manhattan feeling
- Worth ~2 hours total

**Afternoon:** East Village
- Tompkins Square Park → St. Marks Place → 2nd Ave
- Cheap food everywhere, record stores, lots of texture

**Evening:** Dinner in East Village or back to Flushing

---

### DAY 12 — July 15 · Flex Day

Use this for whatever didn't happen or wherever you want to return:
- MoMA if you picked Nowadays on July 12
- Second pass through Williamsburg or Bushwick
- Flushing deep dive: Queens Museum, Queens Botanical Garden, more food
- Or just walk with no plan — the best NYC days are often these

---

### DAY 13 — July 16 · Departure (8:30pm flight)

**Morning:** Last walk — return to your favorite spot from the trip

**Afternoon:** Final meal in Flushing (send it off properly)

**~6:00pm:** Head to airport
- JFK: ~30 min from Flushing via LIRR (Jamaica) or Uber
- Build in time — July traffic + airport lines

## VEGAN FOOD

### Flushing (your base — actually excellent)
- Buddhist vegan Chinese restaurants on Main St: full menus, very cheap
- New World Mall food court (basement) — diverse, authentic
- Night market on weekends along Roosevelt Ave
- Far better than the old itinerary gave it credit for

### By neighborhood
| Area | Strength |
|------|----------|
| Williamsburg | Best concentration of dedicated vegan spots |
| Chinatown | Buddhist restaurants, cheap, excellent |
| Chelsea Market | Variety, slightly pricier, convenient |
| West Village | Easy finds, good quality, mid-price |
| East Village | Cheap and solid, lots of options |

## PHILOSOPHY

- Walk neighborhoods, don't tick landmarks
- 7 train is your friend — Queens to Midtown, fast and direct
- Sunsets after 8:30pm in July — evenings are long, use them
- Flushing is a world-class food destination, not a fallback
- The best NYC moments are unplanned — leave room for them
- NYC is best experienced horizontally, not vertically
